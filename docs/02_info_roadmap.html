The 8-step roadmap

The Causal Roadmap (Petersen and van der Laan 2014) is a step-by-step framework that takes you from a substantive research question to a defensible statistical analysis. Every chapter on this site is keyed to one or more of these steps.

Step 1 — State the research question

The target population, the trial arms, the outcome, and the time horizon.

Running example: “What would be the difference in PrEP initiation within X-weeks if all persons with HIV risk received the intervention vs. the standard-of-care?”

Step 2 — Specify the causal model

A hierarchical (multilevel) causal model that names the variables, their temporal order, and the assumed independencies. For our setup: E^c → W → L → M → Y1* → Y2, with the cluster-level intervention A^c affecting L, M, Y2 directly.

Step 3 — Define the causal effect via counterfactuals

\(\Psi^* = \mathbb{E}^*[Y^c(1) - Y^c(0)]\) where \(Y^c(a^c) = \mathbb{P}^*[Y_2(a^c) = 1 \mid Y_1^*(a^c) = 1]\) — the counterfactual probability of starting PrEP among persons with HIV risk under trial arm \(a^c\).

Step 4 — Specify the observed data

\(O^c = (E^c, A^c, W, L, M, Y_1, Y_2)\) per cluster; one row per individual nested within cluster.

Step 5 — Assess identifiability

Translate the causal estimand into a statistical one — and critically evaluate the assumptions that licence the translation. In our example: PrEP uptake is completely ascertained, and the screened are representative of the unscreened conditional on \((W, L)\).

Step 6 — Specify the statistical estimand

Per [Box 1 of the strategy doc]: at the cluster level, \[ Y^c \;=\; \frac{\mathbb{P}(Y_2 = 1)}{\mathbb{E}\!\bigl[\mathbb{P}(Y_1 = 1 \mid M = 1, W, L)\bigr]}. \] The trial-level estimand is then \[ \Psi \;=\; \mathbb{E}\bigl[\mathbb{E}(Y^c \mid A^c = 1, E^c) - \mathbb{E}(Y^c \mid A^c = 0, E^c)\bigr]. \]

Step 7 — Statistical estimation and inference

Two-Stage TMLE. See Chapter 4 for the conceptual basis and Chapter 5 for the code.

Step 8 — Interpret

Translate the statistical estimate back into the language of the research question, attending to any identification assumptions that may be challenged.

Where you are on the roadmap

Most of this site lives in Steps 6–7 — the statistical layer. But the guarantees of Two-Stage TMLE depend on the work you do in Steps 1–5, which is why we’ll keep linking back here as we go.

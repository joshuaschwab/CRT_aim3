This chapter names the three challenges that motivate the methodology developed in Chapters 4–6.

3a. The multilevel-mediation-missing data problem

In CRTs of strategies that aim to expand both reach of an intervention and outcomes among those reached, three features collide:

  1. Hierarchical data — individuals are nested within clusters.
  2. Mediated effect — the intervention’s first action (e.g., screening) mediates its later action (e.g., PrEP uptake).
  3. Missingness on the causal path — underlying eligibility status is observed only among the screened.

Existing approaches that “adjust for differences between screened vs. not screened” block the indirect effect through screening and bias estimates of the overall effectiveness towards the null. This is the problem that motivates the Nakato (2026) ratio-based Stage 1 estimator — see Chapter 6.

Reference: Figure 5 of the strategy doc; Box 1.

3b. Few independent units, small effective sample size

68% of CRTs randomize fewer than 40 clusters. Within-cluster dependence plus the cluster-level intervention itself shrinks the effective sample size further. Common approaches (mixed models, GEE with finite-sample corrections) tend to perform poorly:

# clusters t-test bias GEE+IPW bias TMLE bias
J = 30 −32% −2% −9%

(From simulated CRTs of an average treatment effect of −9% — Table 2 of the strategy doc.) Two-Stage TMLE’s gain comes from (i) Stage-1 within-cluster missing-data adjustment, and (ii) Stage-2 Adaptive Pre-specification of the cluster-level adjustment covariate — see Chapter 4.

3c. Effect heterogeneity

Fewer than 30% of CRTs assess effect heterogeneity well — established subgroups miss data-driven insights, and naïve stratification on a cluster-level modifier (e.g., region) reduces the effective sample to just the clusters with that characteristic. Regression with interaction terms inflates Type-I errors (one simulation: 47.2%).

Note

Year 2 deliverable: a Chapter 7 (or 5b) on data-adaptive identification and evaluation of effect heterogeneity via the cross-validated cluster-level CATE TMLE plus step-down maxT multiple testing — Aim 1B of the R01.
